## Part 1: What Mental Load Actually Is

**Mental load is NOT chores.**
Chores are VISIBLE (cooking, cleaning).
Mental load is INVISIBLE (planning, remembering).

<div class="comparison-grid">

## Part 4: The Equalization Protocol

### Phase 1: The Audit (Week 1)
1. List EVERY recurring task.
2. Note who currently "owns" it (tracks it).
3. Show your partner the list.

### Phase 2: Divide Ownership (Week 2)
**This is NOT about dividing tasks. It's about dividing OWNERSHIP.**

<div class="highlight-box">
<strong>Ownership means:</strong>
* You track it
* You anticipate it
* You execute it
* You NEVER need reminders
</div>

**Rules for Division:**
1. **Full Ownership:** You don't "co-own."
2. **No Reminding:** If it's their area, you SHUT UP.
3. **Natural Consequences:** If they fail, they deal with the fallout.

### Phase 3: The Transition (Weeks 3-8)

<div class="comparison-grid">
<div>
<strong>For The Manager:</strong>
1. <strong>Let go.</strong> Stop tracking their tasks.
2. <strong>Don't rescue.</strong> Let them fail.
3. <strong>Bite your tongue.</strong> Don't criticize their method.
</div>
<div>
<strong>For The Employee:</strong>
1. <strong>Actually OWN it.</strong> Set your own reminders.
2. <strong>Don't ask for help.</strong> Figure it out.
3. <strong>Anticipate.</strong> Don't wait to be told.
</div>
</div>

## Part 5: Common Obstacles

### Obstacle #1: "But I Don't Notice Things"
**Translation:** "I've never had to, because YOU always handle it."
**Solution:** Noticing is a skill. Practice it.

### Obstacle #2: "You're Better at It"
**Translation:** "I don't want to put in the effort."
**Solution:** Competence comes from practice.

### Obstacle #3: "You'll Just Criticize Me"
**Solution:** Agree on standards upfront. If they meet the standard, NO criticism allowed.
