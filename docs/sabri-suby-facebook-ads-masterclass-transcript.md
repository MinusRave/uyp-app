## 16. Writing the Body Copy: Rules and Principles

### Character Limit

Keep your ad to **2,200 characters** — the maximum Facebook allows to run on both Facebook and Instagram. Write the copy first, disregard length, then edit aggressively to get it within 2,200. Don't constantly watch the character count while writing. Write the best ad you can; if it's under, that's fine. Typically longer copy works better.

### Use the Hemingway App

Run your copy through the Hemingway App to check readability and reading grade level. The best-selling New York Times books and the largest YouTube channels all write at a fifth grade level or below. I personally shoot for below a three or four.

The easier it is for people to read your copy, the more people will read it — and **consumption precedes conversion.** You don't want people to notice your writing. You want them to be moved by your message.

### Copy Rules

- **Short and simple** — words, sentences, and paragraphs. Keep it short and sharp.
- **Line breaks** — lots of them. I get flack for line-by-line copy, but I've split tested it and it works. It gives the user eye relief.
- **Write like you talk.**
- **Make your advertising valuable.** Don't just sell. Provide findings, news, something of value people would want to share.
- **Do not let your ads look like ads** by any means necessary.
- **Sell the click, not the product or service.**
- **Use one and two syllable words** to improve readability.
- **Write "you" not "our customer."** You're writing to one person, not a group.
- **Write "we" or "us" not your company name in the third person.**

### Theme Everything Around a Winning Concept

Once you find a winning headline, make sure the creative is aligned to that headline, the lead-in copy is aligned, the body copy is aligned. Fully commit to one angle.

### Add Zest, Personality, and Specificity

Specificity adds depth to your claims. Don't be afraid to show personality. The more generalized your claims, the more doubtful your readers get.

- DON'T say: "Here's how to lose weight."
- DO say: "Here's how to lose 12 to 19 kilos in the next 5 weeks."
- DON'T say: "Explode your leads."
- DO say: "Here's how to generate 90 to 480 inbound leads every single month."

Specificity increases believability.

### Mobile-First

Use lots of line breaks so it's easy to read on mobile — that's where 80–90% of people are reading. Every sentence needs to be in order, a nice greased slope so people read with ease all the way through.

### Avoid Overusing "You" and "Your"

Ad platforms typically don't like you calling out people directly. As a general rule, don't use "you" and "your" excessively — it can trigger platform restrictions.
