    How many times per day you panic about silence. What triggers the panic (specific situations, times of day, topics). Whether your partner actually was pulling away or whether your anxiety created the distance. Notice if your partner seems less withdrawn when you don't pursue them.

    Success Metric

    By end of week 1, you should have at least 3 instances where you felt panic, self-soothed, and your partner remained engaged (they didn't actually pull away). You should feel noticeably less exhausted from managing your own anxiety.

    Week 2
    Weekly Focus

    Stop being the manager/parent and start being a partner by having ONE conversation about how you actually want to divide responsibilities

    Daily Action

    Spend 10 minutes each day noticing where you're managing, controlling, or parenting your partner. Write down 3-5 specific examples (reminding them about appointments, organizing their things, managing their emotions, etc.). By Thursday, choose ONE thing you're going to stop doing. On Friday, have the conversation using the script from Situation 2: 'I've been carrying a lot, and I've been doing it quietly...'

    What to Track

    How much energy you free up when you stop managing one thing. Whether your partner rises to the occasion or whether the thing doesn't get done (and whether that's actually your problem). How your partner responds when you position this as a partnership redesign, not an accusation.

    Success Metric

    You have the conversation by Friday. Your partner agrees to take on at least one thing you've been managing. You feel noticeably less resentful, even if the thing isn't done perfectly. You notice your partner treating you slightly differently — with less defensiveness, more collaboration.

    Week 3
    Weekly Focus

    Rebuild emotional intimacy (not sexual intimacy yet) by having one unstructured conversation with your partner where you're genuinely curious about them

    Daily Action

    Each evening, ask your partner one question that is NOT about logistics, problems, or the relationship. Examples: 'What made you laugh today?' 'What's something you've been thinking about?' 'If you could do anything this weekend, what would it be?' Listen to the answer without problem-solving or redirecting to yourself. Do not use this conversation to lead into sex. Just let it be connection.

    What to Track

    Whether your partner opens up more when you're not demanding reassurance. Whether you feel more connected to them as a person. Whether the 'roommate' feeling starts to shift. Notice your own urge to turn the conversation into an opportunity to be heard — resist it this week.

    Success Metric

    By end of week 3, you've had 7 of these conversations. Your partner is sharing more. You feel like you're remembering who they are as a person, not just as someone who manages your anxiety. The intimacy score hasn't changed on paper, but it feels different in the room.

    Week 4
    Weekly Focus

    Establish a new rhythm around physical affection and sex that is based on connection, not management

    Daily Action

    Every single day, initiate one small physical touch that is NOT sexual and NOT transactional: hold their hand while watching TV, hug them when you see them, touch their arm when you're talking. Do NOT do this as a way to earn sex or manage their mood. Do it because you're rebuilding the physical safety between you. If your partner initiates sex, use the script from Situation 3 if you're not feeling connected. If you ARE feeling connected after three weeks of emotional intimacy work, say yes and notice the difference.

    What to Track

    Whether your partner reciprocates the small touches. Whether sex, when it happens, feels different (more present, less transactional). Whether the spark (40) shows any signs of life. Notice your guilt about rest — it should be significantly lower by now.

    Success Metric

    You're touching affectionately every day. You've had at least one sexual encounter that felt genuinely connected, not obligatory. Your burnout score should feel slightly lower (you're resting without guilt, you're not managing as much, you're sleeping better). Most importantly: you and your partner are in the same room together, not just occupying the same space.
